Datapalooza Denver - Mini Hack

Some folks believe Global Warming is a myth, while others say it is a fact. Perform some data analysis and support a theory (Global Warming - Myth or Fact) using geological surface temperature data dating from 1750.

As a bonus, identify a region of the earth that has experienced the most significant temperature change and correlate this finding to some external factors to enhance your position.

Top 2 participants with the best analysis win an Apple Watch.

So how do I win an Apple Watch?

You can hack away throughout the day in our dedicated Datapalooza Hack Lounge in House 1. We will also have dedicated rapid hack time from 4:00 - 5:00 pm in House 1, 2 and 3.

Send @antonio (on Slack) a direct message with your Data Scientist Workbench Jupyter notebook link before 5:00 PM to be considered for the top 10.

Top 10 finalists will be announced at 6:00 PM at the after party at Galvanize. Each finalist will have 2 minutes to explain their climate analysis. Our judges will select the top 2 based on the criteria explained below.

Submission process

  1. Create a link for your Data Scientist Workbench notebook. See how in this video.
  2. Send @antonio your link in Slack by 5:00 PM.

Judging Criteria

  • Use of the Data Scientist Workbench and Apache Spark
  • Data Analysis Approach
  • Communication of your results
  • Effectiveness of your Analysis to prove your position

Prizes

Top 2 win an Apple Watch.

Rules

  1. Individual participation (no teams).
  2. Sign up with Data Scientist Workbench before 1:00 PM.
  3. You must use the global land surface temperature dataset provided below. Other datasets of your choosing can be used (in addition) at your discretion. You can import data in your workbench by simply drag and dropping the .csv files on to your workbench.
  4. Use Data Scientist Workbench as the development and demonstration platform (Jupyter Notebook recommended over Zeppelin Notebooks for their convenience when sharing your results.)
  5. Submit your Data Scientist Workbench link before 5:00 PM by sending it to @antonio in the IBM-Datapalooza Slack channel.
  6. If selected as a top 10 finalist, present your solution to a panel of judges (a 2 minute rapid pitch).
  7. Do not share your notebook with others in Slack. Only send it to @antonio to prevent others from "taking inspiration" from your work.

Raw Data

GlobalTemperatures.csv - Global Land and Ocean-and-Land Temperatures

Fields:

  • Dt - starts in 1750 for average land temperature and 1850 for max and min land temperatures and global ocean and land temperatures
  • LandAverageTemperature - global average land temperature in Celsius
  • LandAverageTemperatureUncertainty - the 95% confidence interval around the average
  • LandMaxTemperature - global average maximum land temperature in Celsius
  • LandMaxTemperatureUncertainty - the 95% confidence interval around the maximum land temperature
  • LandMinTemperature - global average minimum land temperature in Celsius
  • LandMinTemperatureUncertainty - the 95% confidence interval around the minimum land temperature
  • LandAndOceanAverageTemperature - global average land and ocean temperature in Celsius
  • LandAndOceanAverageTemperatureUncertainty - the 95% confidence interval around the global average land and ocean temperature

GlobalLandTemperaturesByMajorCity.csv

Fields:

  • Dt
  • AverageTemperature
  • AverageTemperatureUncertainty
  • City
  • Country
  • Latitude
  • Longitude

GlobalLandTemperaturesByCountry.csv

Fields:

  • Dt
  • AverageTemperature
  • AverageTemperatureUncertainty
  • Country
